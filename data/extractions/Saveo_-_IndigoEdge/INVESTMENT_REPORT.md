## Executive Summary

**Verdict:** Hold

**Top 3 Reasons**
1. **Proven Unit Economics in Core Hubs:** Bengaluru hub has achieved positive Contribution Margin 3 (+0.6%), validating the 'dark store' model and logistics efficiency.
2. **Clear Path to Profitability via Private Labels:** The Carin private label brand offers a massive margin uplift (target 65% GM vs. 6-7% for branded generics), which is critical for long-term ROCE.
3. **Execution Risk in Scaling:** Replicating the playbook across 30+ new hubs while managing supplier inventory systems and 3PL dependencies introduces significant operational complexity.

## Strategic Fit & Market Opportunity

Saveo addresses the fragmented B2B pharma distribution market in India by consolidating supply chains through a hub-and-spoke 'dark store' model. The opportunity lies in shifting the category mix from low-margin branded generics to high-margin private labels (Carin) and non-branded generics.

*   **Market Size & Growth:** The company targets USD 750 Mn GMV ARR by 2027, indicating a large addressable market.
*   **Competitive Moat:** High fill rates (97%), rapid delivery (4 hours in small towns), and negative working capital cycle (-3 days in steady state) create operational barriers to entry.
*   **Expansion Strategy:** The playbook is proven in Bengaluru (GMV ARR: $15.9 Mn) and Hyderabad (GMV ARR: $4.0 Mn). The current raise aims to replicate this model across 30+ new hubs.

## Operational Due Diligence

*   **Logistics & Fulfillment:** The 'dark store' strategy enables 97% fill rates and 4-hour delivery in small towns, 24-hour statewide. However, fill rates are slightly below target due to picking errors.
*   **Supplier & Inventory Management:** Supplier inventory systems are not robust enough to prioritize orders effectively, leading to data flow breakdowns post-distributors. This is a critical operational bottleneck.
*   **Customer Retention:** Pharmacy retention is 50%, but GMV retention among retained pharmacies is 110%, indicating strong engagement from core users.
*   **Private Label (Carin):** Carin GMV grew 10x in 10 months (Nov-21 to Sep-22). Current GM is 45%, with a target of 65% peak. This is the key lever for margin expansion.

## Financial Deep Dive

**Historical Facts (Apr 2021 - Sep 2022)**
*   **Revenue Growth:** Net revenue grew from INR 18.26M to INR 165.64M (Sep 2022). GMV grew 16x over the same period.
*   **Margin Improvement:** Gross margin improved from 4.3% to 10.5%. Contribution Margin 3 moved from -15.2% to +0.6% in Bengaluru.
*   **Unit Economics:** Take rate increased from 10% to 13%. AOV is INR 3,052.97 with 9.79 orders per retailer.
*   **Cash Burn:** Monthly burn is INR 2.4 Crs. Operational EBITDA margin is -15% (Sep 2022). Cash balance fluctuated, dropping to INR 14.68M in Dec 2021 before recovering.

**Inferred Conclusions**
*   **Path to Zero Burn:** The company targets zero monthly burn within 11 months. This is achievable if Carin GMV scales as projected and operational efficiencies in new hubs match Bengaluru's performance.
*   **Working Capital:** The model targets negative working capital (-3 days), which will improve cash flow dynamics significantly once scaled.
*   **Profitability Dependency:** Profitability is heavily dependent on the success of the Carin private label. If branded generics remain dominant, margins will stay compressed.

## Risk Matrix (Top 5 Risks)

1.  **Execution Risk in Hub Expansion:** Replicating the Bengaluru playbook across 30+ hubs is complex. *Mitigation:* Validate unit economics in 2-3 new hubs before full-scale rollout.
2.  **Supplier Inventory & Data Flow:** Lack of robust supplier systems leads to picking errors and data breakdowns. *Mitigation:* Invest in supplier integration tech or negotiate better data sharing agreements.
3.  **Margin Pressure from Competition:** The fragmented pharma distribution market is competitive. *Mitigation:* Leverage Carin private label to differentiate and protect margins.
4.  **3PL Dependency:** Wholesale fulfillment relies on third-party logistics. *Mitigation:* Diversify 3PL partners and consider building in-house logistics for high-volume hubs.
5.  **Regulatory Risks:** Pharma supply chain is heavily regulated. *Mitigation:* Ensure strict compliance with drug licensing and distribution regulations.

## Valuation & Exit Range

*   **Valuation:** Cannot be anchored at this stage. The company is raising USD 12-15M, but no pre-money valuation is provided. Given the strong growth and improving unit economics, a premium valuation is likely, but it depends on the current cap table and investor terms.
*   **Exit Range:** Potential exits include strategic acquisition by larger pharma distributors (e.g., Netmeds, Entero, PharmEasy) or IPO if the company scales to USD 750 Mn GMV ARR by 2027.

## Red Flags & Warning Signs

*   **Persistent Operational Losses:** EBITDA margin is -15%. While improving, the company is not yet profitable.
*   **High Cash Burn:** Monthly burn of INR 2.4 Crs requires careful cash management. The company needs to reach zero burn within 11 months to avoid further fundraising.
*   **Wholesale Margin Pressure:** The wholesale segment has significantly lower take rates and margins, dragging down overall profitability.
*   **Pro Forma Data Reliance:** Recent financials are 'Pro Forma' (P) data, which may differ from audited figures. This introduces uncertainty in financial projections.
