## Executive Summary

**Verdict:** Buy

**Top 3 Reasons**
1. **Strong Market Position & Growth:** TenderCuts is India's first and largest omnichannel fresh meat and seafood brand, with a strong presence in Chennai and Hyderabad and rapid expansion into Bangalore. The company has demonstrated robust growth, achieving $30M ARR as of March 2022, with a target of $470M by FY27.
2. **Unique Value Proposition & Technology:** TenderCuts differentiates itself with a 'Farm-to-Fork' approach, delivering truly fresh, cut-to-order meat within 2 hours of slaughter. The company leverages deep technology integration, including proprietary inventory management and AI analytics, to optimize operations and enhance customer experience.
3. **High Customer Retention & Subscription Model:** TenderCuts has a strong focus on customer acquisition and retention, with an 80% repeat sales rate and a unique subscription program ('Tendercuts Elite') that has shown a 50% increase in orders per user post-subscription. This high retention rate and subscription model provide a stable revenue base and enhance customer lifetime value.

## Strategic Fit & Market Opportunity

TenderCuts operates in the $75B Indian meat market, which is predominantly non-vegetarian and largely unorganized. The company's omnichannel model, combining offline stores and online delivery, positions it well to capture a significant share of this market. TenderCuts' focus on fresh, high-quality meat and seafood, combined with its technology-driven operations, addresses a key consumer pain point: the lack of reliable, fresh meat options in the unorganized sector. The company's expansion plans to 10 cities by 2024 further enhance its market opportunity.

## Operational Due Diligence

TenderCuts' operational model is heavily reliant on its supply chain and cold chain logistics. The company's 'Farm-to-Fork' approach requires strict management of perishable goods, which presents both an opportunity and a challenge. TenderCuts' technology stack, including proprietary inventory management and last-mile delivery tech, helps mitigate some of these risks. However, the company's reliance on third-party delivery partners and the complexity of its SKU portfolio pose operational challenges. The company's focus on ESG and ethical sourcing further enhances its operational credibility.

- TenderCuts' subscription program ('Tendercuts Elite') has shown a 50% increase in orders per user post-subscription, indicating strong customer engagement and potential for revenue growth.

## Risk Matrix (Top 5 Risks)

1. **Supply Chain & Cold Chain Complexity:** TenderCuts' reliance on perishable goods requires strict management of cold chain logistics. Any disruption in the supply chain could lead to significant wastage and operational inefficiencies.
   - **Why it matters:** High dependency on perishable goods leads to potential dump and wastage costs, impacting margins.
   - **Mitigation / Validation Ask:** Validate TenderCuts' cold chain infrastructure and waste management processes. Assess the company's contingency plans for supply chain disruptions.

2. **High Initial CAC & Competition:** TenderCuts faces competition from well-funded peers like Licious, which have significantly higher funding and market presence. High initial CAC could impact unit economics.
   - **Why it matters:** High CAC ($3.5) vs. AOV (₹410-550) and low repeat rate (15%) make breakeven difficult without scale.
   - **Mitigation / Validation Ask:** Analyze TenderCuts' CAC trends and customer acquisition strategies. Assess the company's ability to maintain high retention rates and reduce CAC over time.

3. **Execution Risk in Scaling:** TenderCuts plans to expand to 10 cities by 2024, which presents significant execution risks.
   - **Why it matters:** Execution risk in scaling from 3 cities to 10 cities by 2024, including maintaining unit economics and operational efficiency.
   - **Mitigation / Validation Ask:** Review TenderCuts' expansion plans and operational readiness. Assess the company's ability to replicate its success in new markets.

4. **Data Inconsistencies & Reporting Gaps:** There are data inconsistencies in TenderCuts' financial and operational reporting, including gaps in cohort retention tables and revenue projections.
   - **Why it matters:** Data inconsistencies could indicate underlying issues with the company's financial reporting and operational metrics.
   - **Mitigation / Validation Ask:** Request detailed financial and operational data from TenderCuts to validate the reported metrics. Assess the company's data management and reporting processes.

5. **Regulatory & Compliance Risks:** TenderCuts operates in a highly regulated industry, with compliance requirements related to meat processing and hygiene standards.
   - **Why it matters:** Regulatory risks associated with meat processing and hygiene standards could impact the company's operations and reputation.
   - **Mitigation / Validation Ask:** Review TenderCuts' compliance with regulatory requirements. Assess the company's risk management processes and contingency plans for regulatory changes.
