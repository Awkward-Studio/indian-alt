## Executive Summary

**Verdict:** Hold

**Top 3 Reasons**
1. **Strong Unit Economics & Tech-Enabled Model:** Light Microfinance demonstrates robust operational efficiency with a high rejection rate (70%) ensuring asset quality, and a tech stack (SAP/AWS) that supports scalable underwriting and collections.
2. **Clear Growth Trajectory in Under-Penetrated Markets:** The company has a clear expansion plan into Haryana and MP, targeting a 3x book value valuation and INR 1,000 Cr+ AUM by FY23.
3. **Significant Data Integrity & Model Risks:** The financial model is described as 'hard-coded' with logical gaps, and there are concerns over off-book AUM, branch profitability, and the aggressive nature of growth projections.

## Strategic Fit & Market Opportunity

Light Microfinance aligns with India Alternatives' focus on financial inclusion and women's empowerment. The company targets rural women borrowers in Gujarat and Rajasthan, which are high-potential but under-penetrated markets. The shift towards digital lending and micro-enterprise financing offers a TAM of ~₹45 trillion. The company's tech-first approach differentiates it from traditional MFIs, allowing for lower operational costs and faster turnaround times.

## Operational Due Diligence

*   **Underwriting:** Rigorous JLG model with a 70% rejection rate. Proprietary AI-based credit scoring.
*   **Collections:** Smartphone-first approach with high collection efficiency (95-97%).
*   **Technology:** Built on SAP and AWS. Mobile app for customers.
*   **Human Capital:** 1,222 employees, 131 branches. High attrition in senior management (12.5% in FY20) and loan officers (34.7% in FY21) is a concern, though below industry benchmarks.
*   **Governance:** Statutory auditors reported cash embezzlement (₹12.66 lakhs in FY20, ₹7.97 lakhs in FY21). Organizational chart has typographical errors and inconsistencies.

## Financial Deep Dive

*   **Historical Performance:**
    *   FY20 Revenue: ₹76.85 Cr (75% YoY growth); PAT: ₹4.02 Cr (114% YoY growth).
    *   FY21 Revenue: ₹112.26 Cr; PAT: ₹12.73 Cr; AUM: ₹623 Cr.
    *   NIM: 11.5% (FY21); Cost of Funds: 12.1% (FY21).
*   **Projected Performance:**
    *   FY23E AUM: ₹1,782 Cr; PAT: ₹27 Cr; ROE: 12.0%.
    *   FY28E AUM: ₹19,672 Cr; PAT: ₹784 Cr; ROE: 18.5%.
*   **Inferred Conclusions:**
    *   The projected AUM CAGR of 68% (FY18-FY23) is aggressive and lacks clear justification regarding loan officer productivity.
    *   The financial model is 'hard-coded' and lacks logical links between disbursal, employee productivity, and balance sheet assumptions.
    *   Off-book AUM of ₹125 Cr in FY21 requires clarification on ownership and accounting treatment.

## Risk Matrix (Top 5 Risks)

1.  **Model Integrity & Projection Validity:** The financial model is hard-coded and lacks logical links. Projections for Haryana (₹80 Cr in Year 1) are inconsistent with historical scaling in MP (₹27 Cr over 3 years).
2.  **Asset Quality & Off-Book AUM:** High PAR in top-up loans (6.81%) vs. renewal loans (2.15%). ₹125 Cr in off-book AUM raises concerns about true asset quality and regulatory compliance.
3.  **Operational Scalability & Attrition:** High attrition in senior management and loan officers. Rapid expansion of loan officers (1,089 to 4,194) may strain operational efficiency.
4.  **Regulatory & Compliance Risks:** RBI regulations on MFI ticket sizes and interest rates. NDA ambiguities (jurisdiction, stamp duty, entity naming) pose legal risks.
5.  **Governance & Internal Controls:** Instances of cash embezzlement and organizational chart errors suggest weak internal controls and governance.

## Valuation & Exit Range

*   **Target Valuation:** 3x Book Value.
*   **Current Book Value:** ~₹76 Cr (Series A Net Worth: ₹38 Cr).
*   **Implied Valuation:** ~₹228 Cr.
*   **Exit Range:** Not explicitly defined. Comparable transactions (Arohan, Ujjivan) suggest P/E multiples of 1.74x - 2.75x. Given the risks, a conservative multiple should be applied.

## Red Flags & Warning Signs

*   **Hard-Coded Financial Model:** Lack of logical links between key drivers.
*   **Off-Book AUM:** ₹125 Cr in off-book assets with unclear accounting.
*   **Cash Embezzlement:** Statutory auditors reported instances of cash misappropriation.
*   **NDA Ambiguities:** Jurisdiction, stamp duty, and entity naming issues in the NDA.
*   **Aggressive Projections:** Haryana target of ₹80 Cr in Year 1 vs. MP's ₹27 Cr over 3 years.

## Next Steps / Data Requests

1.  **Financial Model Review:** Request a transparent, driver-based financial model with clear links between disbursal, employee productivity, and balance sheet assumptions.
2.  **Off-Book AUM Clarification:** Detailed breakdown of the ₹125 Cr off-book AUM, including ownership, accounting treatment, and risk profile.
3.  **Branch-Level P&L:** Granular data on branch profitability, including break-even timelines for new branches.
4.  **Governance & Internal Controls:** Report on the cash embezzlement incidents and steps taken to strengthen internal controls.
5.  **NDA Resolution:** Clarify and rectify the ambiguities in the NDA (jurisdiction, stamp duty, entity naming).
6.  **Loan Officer Productivity:** Data on loan officer productivity and how it supports the aggressive growth projections.
7.  **Regulatory Compliance:** Confirmation of compliance with RBI regulations on MFI ticket sizes and interest rates.
