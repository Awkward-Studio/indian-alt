## Strategic Fit & Market Opportunity

Zunroof Group operates across three distinct verticals, targeting the clean-tech and smart home markets:

- **Zunroof (Solar Installation):** The largest vertical with INR 35 Cr revenue. It has solarized 5,500 houses with an average ticket size of INR 1.6-1.8 Lacs. The market is growing, but the business model is heavily tied to government subsidies, which introduces regulatory risk and working capital inefficiencies.
- **Zunsolar (Inverters & Batteries):** A smaller vertical with INR 5 Cr revenue and 300-350 distributors. It operates in a market dominated by large players like Luminous (INR 2,500 Cr revenue), leaving limited room for product innovation and market share gains.
- **Zunpulse (Smart IoT Devices):** A D2C smart electricity brand leveraging IoT and ML. It has a run rate of INR 6 Cr with a 25% gross margin. The Indian smart home market is projected to grow at a 38% CAGR, reaching USD 12.1 Bn by 2025. However, the company faces challenges with bad reviews and supply chain dependencies.

## Operational Due Diligence

- **Management & Leadership:** The company is led by 'Jone' and is backed by Godrej family investors. The leadership's decision to cap growth due to subsidy reliance is a red flag for an investment seeking high returns.
- **Supply Chain:** Zunpulse relies on imported supply chains from China, which poses risks related to tariffs, logistics, and geopolitical tensions. This dependency could impact margins and product availability.
- **Sales & Distribution:** Zunpulse has a 60/40 online/offline sales mix. The reliance on online channels exposes the company to platform risks and customer acquisition costs, while the offline channel requires significant operational overhead.
- **Regulatory & Compliance:** The company is described as 'E-BIRDA born' and is in 'B5' status, which may indicate specific regulatory classifications or restrictions. This needs further clarification to assess compliance risks.
