## Risk Matrix (Top 5 Risks)

1. **Credit Risk:** MHIL's exposure to low-to-middle-income salaried customers and migrant workers in specific sectors (diamond and textile) and geographies (Surat, Gujarat) poses a credit risk. The company's aggressive provisioning policy has helped mitigate this risk, but a sustained economic downturn could lead to higher defaults.
2. **Geographical Concentration:** MHIL has a significant concentration of branches in Western and Northern India, with Maharashtra and Gujarat accounting for ~40% of its branches. This geographical concentration poses a risk if the economic conditions in these regions deteriorate.
3. **Dependency on Parent Company:** MHIL relies heavily on MFL for customer sourcing and branch infrastructure. Any disruption in this relationship could impact MHIL's growth and operational efficiency.
4. **Interest Rate Risk:** MHIL's profitability is sensitive to interest rate fluctuations. A rise in interest rates could increase the company's cost of funds and compress its net interest margin (NIM).
5. **Execution Risk:** MHIL's ambitious growth projections require successful execution of its branch expansion plans and integration of new technology systems. Any delays or challenges in execution could impact the company's ability to achieve its growth targets.
