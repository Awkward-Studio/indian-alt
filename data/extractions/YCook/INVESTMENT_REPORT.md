## Executive Summary

**Verdict:** Hold

**Top 3 Reasons**
1. **Strong Market Tailwinds:** Y-Cook operates in the rapidly growing healthy snacking segment, leveraging a unique farm-to-shelf model with thermal sterilization technology that eliminates the need for preservatives.
2. **Scalable Distribution & Farmer Network:** The company has established a network of ~2,500 partner farmers and distributes across 13,700 stores, providing a solid foundation for scaling.
3. **Significant Execution Risks:** Current EBITDA is deeply negative (-INR 12 Cr in FY20), capacity utilization is low (~20%), and the company faces management gaps and unproven expansion into new product categories.

## Strategic Fit & Market Opportunity

Y-Cook is positioned in the healthy snacking market, a segment experiencing robust growth in India. The company's unique value proposition lies in its ability to produce steamed snacks without preservatives using thermal sterilization technology, appealing to health-conscious consumers.

- **Market Size:** The Indian snacking industry is growing, with a specific shift towards healthier options.
- **Product Strategy:** Primarily focused on steamed corn, with plans to expand into tubers and beans. However, new categories currently contribute insignificant volume.
- **Distribution:** Currently distributed across 13,700 stores, with a target to scale to 40,000+ stores.

## Operational Due Diligence

- **Farm-to-Shelf Model:** Y-Cook works with ~2,500 partner farmers who own ~4,500 acres of land, harvesting ~86,000 tons of fresh produce annually.
- **Production:** The company operates a ~53,500 sq. ft. production facility with a capacity of ~25,00,000 units. Current capacity utilization is low at ~20%.
- **Management:** The founding team is experienced, but second-tier management stability is noted. There are identified management gaps that need to be addressed.
- **R&D:** Strong R&D capabilities are highlighted, supporting product innovation and quality.

## Financial Deep Dive

**Historical Facts:**
- **FY20 Revenue:** INR 25.5 Cr (INR 254 Mn)
- **FY20 EBITDA:** -INR 12 Cr
- **Gross Margin:** 33%
- **Funds Raised to Date:** INR 56 Cr
- **Monthly Run Rate (Dec 2020):** INR 25 Mn (Targeting INR 45 Mn by March 2021)

**Inferred Conclusions:**
- **Burn Rate:** The negative EBITDA is driven by marketing and employee expenses, indicating a high burn rate relative to revenue.
- **Growth Trajectory:** The company targets INR 33 Cr revenue in FY21 and INR 85-90 Cr in FY22, aiming for INR 300 Cr in 4-5 years. This implies a CAGR of ~67% from FY20 to FY25E.
- **Margin Improvement:** Projected operating margins are expected to improve to 22% by FY25E, with PAT margins at 10%.

## Risk Matrix (Top 5 Risks)

1. **Negative EBITDA & High Burn:** Current EBITDA is -INR 12 Cr, driven by marketing and employee expenses. *Mitigation:* Validate burn rate efficiency and path to breakeven.
2. **Low Capacity Utilization:** Only ~20% of production capacity is utilized. *Mitigation:* Assess demand generation strategies and sales pipeline.
3. **Farmer Relations:** Lack of guaranteed purchase arrangements with farmers. *Mitigation:* Review farmer contracts and supply chain stability.
4. **Management Gaps:** Identified gaps in management, though second-tier is stable. *Mitigation:* Evaluate management hiring plan and organizational structure.
5. **New Product Categories:** Expansion into tubers and sprouts has insignificant volume. *Mitigation:* Assess market demand and R&D progress for new categories.

## Valuation & Exit Range

Valuation cannot yet be anchored due to limited evidence on current post-money valuation and comparable transactions. The company is seeking USD 7 Mn for secondary buyouts and distribution expansion. Historical funds raised total INR 56 Cr. Promoter shareholding is 31.5%, and Omnivore holds 31.3%.

## Red Flags & Warning Signs

- **Jurisdiction Mismatch:** NDAs mandate Mumbai courts for a Bangalore-based entity, increasing legal costs.
- **Unilateral Commitment:** NDA structure suggests potential ambiguity in obligations.
- **Broad 'Representatives' Definition:** Expands liability scope for inadvertent disclosure.
- **Low Capacity Utilization:** Indicates potential overcapacity or demand challenges.
- **Historical Losses:** Negative ROCE and PAT in FY20A and projected losses until FY23E.
