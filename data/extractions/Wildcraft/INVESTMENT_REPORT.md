## Executive Summary

**Verdict:** Hold

**Top 3 Reasons**
1. **High Growth Potential in Fragmented Market:** Wildcraft is the only organized integrated outdoor player in India, targeting a massive addressable market (USD 2.5 Bn) with low current penetration (<1% serious enthusiasts). The company has a dominant 40% share in online equipment sales and a scalable EBO model.
2. **Aggressive Expansion Plan:** The USD 30M raise will fund a near-doubling of the EBO network (130 to 298 stores) and working capital for apparel/footwear categories. CIM projects revenue growth from INR 2,393 Mn (2017) to INR 12,450 Mn (2023) with EBITDA margins expanding from 2% to 20%.
3. **Significant Operational and Financial Risks:** The company has a history of net losses (INR 129.56 Mn in FY16-17), negative operating cash flow, and high inventory levels. Audit qualifications regarding revenue recognition and internal controls raise concerns about financial reporting quality. High working capital intensity and dependence on short-term borrowings for liquidity add to the risk profile.

## Strategic Fit & Market Opportunity

Wildcraft is positioned as India's leading integrated outdoor player, covering gear, clothing, and footwear. The company targets both 'hard' outdoor enthusiasts and 'soft' urban consumers, adopting a 'generalist' approach similar to global leaders like The North Face and Columbia.

*   **Market Size:** The India addressable outdoor market is estimated at USD 2.5 Bn (Apparel: USD 900 Mn, Footwear: USD 620 Mn, Travel Accessories: USD 990 Mn). The global adventure travel market is valued at USD 89 Bn.
*   **Growth Drivers:** Rising disposable incomes, urbanization, and a shift from utilitarian to lifestyle products. E-commerce is a high-growth channel, with Wildcraft holding a 40% market share in online equipment sales.
*   **Competitive Landscape:** The market is highly fragmented and unorganized (70% unorganized in travel accessories). Wildcraft faces competition from global giants (Nike, Adidas, The North Face) in crossover segments and local players offering lower price points. Wildcraft's pricing is 20-25% cheaper than The North Face, providing a competitive edge.
*   **Strategic Focus:** Expansion of the EBO network from 130 to 298 stores by FY23, strengthening Modern Trade relationships, and scaling the online channel. The company is also expanding into apparel and footwear, which are higher-margin categories.

## Operational Due Diligence

*   **Business Model:** Multi-channel strategy including EBOs (Owned and Franchise), Modern Trade (Central, Lifestyle, Big Bazaar), Trade (979 dealers), Online, and Corporate/Institutional.
*   **Distribution Network:**
    *   **EBOs:** 130 stores (FY17), targeting 298 by FY23. Store size mix: <500 sq ft (50%), 500-750 sq ft (33%).
    *   **Modern Trade:** 278 counters, 19 relationships. Revenue share ~21% (FY13).
    *   **Trade:** 20 distributors, 979 dealers. Revenue share ~22% (FY13).
    *   **Online:** 40% market share in online equipment. Revenue share ~7% (FY13).
    *   **Corporate:** 587 active relationships. Top 10 clients contributed 56% of FY13 revenue.
*   **Manufacturing:** >90% in-house manufacturing with a total capacity of 1.28 million pieces/year. Tax benefits in Himachal Pradesh facility expire partially in FY14.
*   **Product Portfolio:** 'Head-to-head' range including gear, clothing, and footwear. Strong R&D capability and a large creation team. Portfolio churn is 75% annually.
*   **Operational Efficiency:** Warehouse fill rate is 99.98%. Gear sell-through at full price is 95%, and apparel sell-through is 85%.
*   **Key Personnel:** Co-founders Siddharth and Gaurav are leading the company. No CFO is currently in place (as of May 2017).

## Financial Deep Dive

*   **Historical Performance:**
    *   **Revenue:** Grew from INR 631.12 Mn (FY13) to INR 2,239.66 Mn (FY16-17). CIM projects revenue growth from INR 2,393 Mn (2017) to INR 12,450 Mn (2023).
    *   **Profitability:** The company has a history of net losses, including INR 129.56 Mn in FY16-17. CIM projects EBITDA margin expansion from 2% (FY17) to 20% (FY23) and PAT recovery from -5.7% (2017) to 11.9% (2023).
    *   **Cash Flow:** Operating cash flow was negative INR 189.75 Mn in FY16-17. Financing cash flow was positive INR 364.18 Mn, indicating reliance on external funding.
*   **Inferred Conclusions:**
    *   **Margin Expansion:** The projected margin expansion is aggressive and relies on scaling the higher-margin apparel and footwear categories, as well as operational efficiencies in the EBO network.
    *   **Working Capital Intensity:** High working capital intensity (peaked at 170 days in FY15-17) due to nascent clothing/footwear categories and inventory buildup. This will require significant funding.
    *   **Channel Mix Shift:** The company is shifting from a gear-centric model to a lifestyle brand, which may impact revenue mix and margin profiles in the short term.

## Risk Matrix (Top 5 Risks)

1.  **Financial Reporting & Audit Qualifications:**
    *   **Why it matters:** Qualified audit opinions in FY15-16 regarding internal financial controls, revenue recognition (AS 9), and lease accounting (AS 19) raise concerns about the reliability of financial statements.
    *   **Mitigation / Validation Ask:** Request detailed management response to audit qualifications. Engage a third-party accountant to review internal controls and financial reporting processes.
2.  **Liquidity & Working Capital Risk:**
    *   **Why it matters:** Negative operating cash flow, high inventory levels (INR 923.38 Mn in FY16-17), and dependence on short-term borrowings (INR 566.63 Mn) indicate liquidity pressure and refinancing risk.
    *   **Mitigation / Validation Ask:** Analyze inventory turnover and aging. Review debt maturity profile and refinancing plans. Assess the impact of working capital requirements on the USD 30M raise.
3.  **Execution Risk in EBO Expansion:**
    *   **Why it matters:** The plan to double the EBO network from 130 to 298 stores by FY23 is aggressive. Negative EBITDA in multiple stores in FY17 indicates location/channel profitability issues.
    *   **Mitigation / Validation Ask:** Conduct store-level profitability analysis. Assess the company's ability to source and train store staff. Review franchisee agreements and brand consistency controls.
4.  **Customer & Channel Concentration:**
    *   **Why it matters:** Top 5 corporate customers accounted for 40% of sales in FY17. Online sales are heavily dependent on Flipkart and Amazon (69% of online sales). Modern Trade receivables average 90 days.
    *   **Mitigation / Validation Ask:** Analyze customer concentration and contract terms. Assess the impact of platform policy changes on online sales. Review Modern Trade payment terms and collection efficiency.
5.  **Competitive Pressure & Market Penetration:**
    *   **Why it matters:** The market is highly fragmented and unorganized. Global giants (Nike, Adidas, The North Face) are expanding in India. Low penetration of serious outdoor enthusiasts (<1%) limits the addressable market.
    *   **Mitigation / Validation Ask:** Conduct competitive analysis and market sizing validation. Assess Wildcraft's brand strength and customer loyalty. Review marketing spend and effectiveness.

## Valuation & Exit Range

*   **Valuation Basis:** SMC Capitals presentation provides an indicative Enterprise Value of INR 2,400 - 3,000 Mn and Equity Value of INR 2,100 - 2,700 Mn, based on a Forward EBITDA of INR 200 Mn and multiples of 12x - 15x (Forward EBITDA) and 2.5x (EV/Revenue).
*   **Comparable Analysis:** Comparable EV/EBITDA median is 13.1x - 14.2x. Comparable EV/Revenue median is 2.5x.
*   **Exit Range:** Given the high growth projections and margin expansion, a potential exit multiple of 15x - 20x Forward EBITDA could be achievable by FY23, assuming successful execution of the expansion plan and market share gains. However, this is highly speculative and dependent on market conditions and company performance.
*   **Caveat:** Valuation is based on 'no exact comparable companies' and forward projections that may differ from reality. The indicative valuation should be treated as a starting point for negotiation.

## Red Flags & Warning Signs

*   **Audit Qualifications:** Qualified audit opinions in FY15-16 regarding internal controls, revenue recognition, and lease accounting.
*   **Net Losses & Negative Cash Flow:** History of net losses and negative operating cash flow, indicating operational challenges.
*   **High Inventory & Receivables:** Significant increase in inventory and trade receivables, raising concerns about collection efficiency and obsolescence risk.
*   **Dependence on Short-Term Borrowings:** High reliance on short-term borrowings for liquidity management, increasing refinancing risk.
*   **No CFO in Place:** Lack of a dedicated CFO as of May 2017 may impact financial governance and reporting quality.
*   **Data Inconsistencies:** Data fragmentation and inconsistencies across multiple pages/tables in the CIM and financial statements.
