## Strategic Fit & Market Opportunity

Keventers operates in the dairy and quick-service restaurant (QSR) sector, with a focus on milkshakes, ice creams, and milk supply. The brand has established a strong presence in North India and is exploring expansion into Tier II/III cities and international markets.

- **Business Model:** Heavily franchise-led. As of June 2018, the company operated 205 franchised outlets, 20 company-owned stores, and 12 joint ventures.
- **Market Position:** The brand is recognized for premium milkshakes and dairy products. The company is shifting towards a D2C and Home Delivery model, which grew to 35% of the sales mix by October 2019.
- **Growth Drivers:** Expansion into new geographies, increasing store count (projected to grow from ~430 to ~620 stores by FY26), and improving same-store sales growth (SSSG) through marketing initiatives like 'Milkshake Mania'.

## Operational Due Diligence

- **Franchise Model:** The franchise fee structure ranges from INR 5-9 lakhs (standard) to INR 18 lakhs (Dubai exception). Franchisee churn rate is low at 8%, well below the acceptable 20% threshold.
- **Home Delivery Shift:** The company has successfully pivoted to include home delivery, which now accounts for 35% of sales. This channel shift is critical for margin improvement and customer reach.
- **Marketing & Customer Acquisition:** The 'Milkshake Mania' campaign improved Net Promoter Score (NPS) to 56.7 and Value for Money (VFM) perception to 76.0%. The customer database has grown to 1.75 million, with 84.1% phone number compliance.
- **Regional Performance:** There is a stark contrast in performance between regions. North and East regions show positive SSSG, while South and West regions continue to struggle with negative growth.

## Financial Deep Dive

**Historical Facts:**
- **Revenue Growth:** Revenue from operations nearly doubled from INR 27.1 Cr in FY17 to INR 61.0 Cr in FY18.
- **Profitability:** Net profit increased from INR 1.7 Cr in FY17 to INR 42.5 Cr in FY18. However, this spike requires scrutiny as it may include one-time items or accounting adjustments.
- **Overhead Inflation:** Monthly corporate overheads increased drastically from INR 50 lakhs to INR 90 lakhs year-over-year.
- **Franchise Advances:** 'Advances from Franchisees' on the balance sheet nearly doubled from INR 39.5 Cr to INR 90.4 Cr, indicating heavy reliance on franchisee funding or deferred revenue recognition.

**Inferred Conclusions:**
- The massive jump in net profit in FY18 is likely driven by aggressive franchise fee recognition and possibly one-time gains, rather than sustainable operational improvements.
- The high corporate overheads relative to EBITDA suggest that the company is spending heavily on scaling before achieving operational leverage.
- The reliance on franchise advances as a liability line item suggests that the company is using franchisee capital to fund operations, which could pose risks if franchisee sentiment turns.
