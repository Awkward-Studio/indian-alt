## Executive Summary

**Verdict:** Hold (with conditions)

**Top 3 Reasons**
1. **Strong Historical Growth & Margins:** Auriga has demonstrated a 23% historical revenue CAGR and consistently high EBITDA margins (>25%), driven by a diversified client base and a strategic pivot towards high-margin bioequivalence (BA/BE) studies.
2. **Regulatory Tailwinds & PPP Model:** The Public-Private Partnership (PPP) with FSSAI for food testing provides a guaranteed revenue floor and de-risks the food segment, while regulatory modernization in India creates a structural growth opportunity.
3. **Execution & Working Capital Risks:** The deal carries significant execution risk related to the Manesar lab expansion and regional rollout. High Capex requirements, negative free cash flow in early years, and increasing debtor days (85 to 117 days) pose liquidity and operational challenges.

## Strategic Fit & Market Opportunity

Auriga operates in the Testing, Inspection, and Certification (TIC) sector, specifically focusing on pharmaceutical and food safety testing. The Indian market is fragmented, with in-house testing accounting for 57% of the market, leaving a 43% opportunity for outsourced players like Auriga.

*   **Market Size & Growth:** The food testing lab market potential is estimated at INR 200 Cr, with a growth ceiling below INR 500 Cr. The pharmaceutical testing market is larger and driven by generic drug exports and regulatory compliance.
*   **Regulatory Tailwinds:** The FSSAI has mandated stricter food safety standards, leading to increased testing volumes. Auriga's PPP model with FSSAI for an appellate laboratory in Ghaziabad guarantees a minimum revenue of INR 25 lakhs/month and reimburses setup Capex, providing a stable cash flow base.
*   **Strategic Expansion:** The company is expanding its hub-and-spoke model with a new central lab in Manesar (INR 20-22 Cr Capex) and six regional labs by FY20. The Manesar lab will focus on high-margin BA/BE studies and pharma testing, while regional labs will target food and export testing near key pharma hubs and ports.
*   **Competitive Landscape:** Auriga faces competition from global giants (Eurofins, SGS, Bureau Veritas) and local players (Vimta Labs, Choksi Labs). Auriga differentiates itself through adaptability, faster turnaround times, and the ability to handle non-standard tests.

## Operational Due Diligence

*   **Facility & Capacity:** Auriga operates four existing labs (Delhi, Baddi, Bangalore, FSSAI Ghaziabad) and plans to launch a central Manesar lab and six regional labs. The Manesar lab will have a 120-bed capacity and peak BA/BE capacity of 12-13 studies per month.
*   **Client Base:** The company serves over 140 clients, primarily in the pharma and FMCG sectors. Key clients include Acme Generics (70% wallet share), Amway India (60%), Mankind Pharma (50%), and Strides Arcolab (40%).
*   **Service Segments:** Primary focus on pharmaceutical testing (70%+ of Bangalore revenue), expanding into high-margin stability testing, food testing (residue testing), and storage services.
*   **Human Capital:** The company faces talent shortages in quality control technicians due to low sector salaries. Technician churn in the Delhi lab was 16 left and 40 joined in the last year.
*   **Operational Efficiency:** The company has shown the ability to turn around underperforming labs (e.g., Bangalore lab turned profitable by FY17 after initial delays). The FSSAI PPP lab benefits from no rental expenses and no repairs/maintenance costs (covered by FSSAI warranties).

## Risk Matrix (Top 5 Risks)

1.  **Execution Risk (Manesar & Regional Labs):**
    *   **Why it matters:** The company plans to scale Manesar operations from 1,200 tests in Year 1 to 20,000+ long-term, and roll out 6 new regional labs by FY20. Failure to achieve these targets will impact revenue projections and cash flow.
    *   **Mitigation/Validation Ask:** Validate the Manesar lab's accreditation timeline (US FDA, WHO GLP) and regional lab site selection. Review detailed project plans and staffing strategies for the new labs.
2.  **Working Capital & Liquidity Pressure:**
    *   **Why it matters:** Debtor days have increased from 85 to 117 days, and free cash flow is projected to be negative in early years due to high Capex. This could strain liquidity and require additional debt financing.
    *   **Mitigation/Validation Ask:** Analyze the aging of receivables and collection policies. Assess the company's debt covenants and ability to secure additional financing if needed.
3.  **Client Concentration & Dependency:**
    *   **Why it matters:** High reliance on key clients like Amway India (shifting to Mangalore) and Mankind Pharma (setting up own R&D). Top clients in Baddi and Bangalore labs account for 77% and 58% of revenue, respectively.
    *   **Mitigation/Validation Ask:** Review client contracts and retention rates. Assess the risk of client attrition and the company's pipeline for new client acquisition.
4.  **Regulatory & Compliance Risks:**
    *   **Why it matters:** Growth in the food sector is dependent on FSSAI regulations and government contracts. Regulatory changes or delays in accreditation (FDA, Malaysian MPPA) could impact operations.
    *   **Mitigation/Validation Ask:** Monitor FSSAI policy updates and accreditation timelines. Review the company's compliance history and any pending regulatory issues.
5.  **Data Integrity & Financial Reporting:**
    *   **Why it matters:** Historical data inconsistencies, such as convictions exceeding cases launched in food safety reports, and discrepancies in ITRs, raise questions about financial reporting quality.
    *   **Mitigation/Validation Ask:** Conduct a thorough forensic audit of historical financials and operational data. Verify the accuracy of revenue recognition and expense allocation.

## Valuation & Exit Range

*   **Valuation:** Valuation cannot yet be anchored due to the lack of explicit deal terms and the high growth projections. However, based on projected FY21 PAT of INR 26.9 Cr and a peer multiple of 15-20x (Vimta Labs trades at 16.5x FY18E P/E), the implied valuation range is INR 400-540 Cr.
*   **Exit Range:** Potential exits include strategic sale to global TIC players (Eurofins, SGS) or domestic peers (Vimta Labs), or an IPO if the company achieves INR 50 Cr+ revenue and consistent profitability.

## Red Flags & Warning Signs

*   **Data Inconsistencies:** Convictions (7,860) exceed cases launched (2,676) in food safety reports, implying historical data inclusion or inconsistency.
*   **Financial Volatility:** Significant fluctuations in R&D and SG&A as % of sales indicate operational instability or accounting anomalies.
*   **Working Capital Pressure:** Debtor days increased significantly (85 to 117 days), suggesting cash flow constraints.
*   **Asset Valuation Discrepancy:** Financial model assumes HPLC machines cost INR 16 lacs, while market data suggests INR 24 lacs, raising questions about asset quality or model accuracy.
*   **Client Dependency:** High reliance on key clients like Amway India (shifting to Mangalore) and Mankind Pharma (setting up own R&D).
