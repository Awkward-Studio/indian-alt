## Executive Summary

**Verdict:** Buy (CAGL) / Hold (AFSL)

**Top 3 Reasons**
1. **Structural Tailwinds & Regulatory Easing:** RBI's removal of spread caps and increase in household income limits to ₹0.3M significantly expands the addressable market for both entities, allowing for risk-based pricing and higher yields.
2. **Post-Pandemic Recovery & Asset Quality Normalization:** Both CAGL and AFSL have demonstrated strong recovery in disbursements and collections post-COVID. CAGL's asset quality is stabilizing with credit costs expected to normalize to 1.8-2.0%, while AFSL's high-yield segments (MSME, 2W) are driving profitability despite temporary NPA spikes.
3. **Digital Transformation & Operational Efficiency:** CAGL's 99% cashless disbursement rate and robust tech stack (Digimeter ratings) provide a competitive moat. AFSL's 'Jayam' digital initiative and focus on high-yield MSME loans offer growth potential, though execution risks remain.

## Strategic Fit & Market Opportunity

*   **Market Size & Growth:** The Indian MFI sector is projected to grow at 18% CAGR over the next 5 years, driven by low rural penetration (38% currently, targeting 53% by FY25). The retail lending opportunity is estimated at ₹1.5T by FY25.
*   **Regulatory Environment:** Recent RBI guidelines allow NBFC-MFIs to lend up to 25% of assets to non-MFI products (up from 10-15%), enabling diversification into MSME and Two-Wheeler finance. This benefits both CAGL and AFSL.
*   **Competitive Landscape:** Banks hold 40% of the MFI portfolio, but NBFC-MFIs are gaining share due to better risk-based underwriting and digital capabilities. CAGL is the largest NBFC-MFI, while AFSL is a key player in Gujarat and MP.
*   **Strategic Initiatives:**
    *   **CAGL:** "Vision 2025" targets 10M households. Aggressive expansion into Gujarat, UP, and Bihar. Integration of Madura Microfinance (MMFL) is ongoing.
    *   **AFSL:** Focus on high-yield MSME and Two-Wheeler segments. Geographic diversification beyond Gujarat into UP, Rajasthan, and MP. Targeting ₹5,000 Cr AUM by FY26.

## Operational Due Diligence

*   **Technology & Digital Adoption:**
    *   **CAGL:** 99% cashless disbursements. Digimeter ratings show 5.0/5.0 for core systems (LOS, LMS, CBS). Strong vendor management.
    *   **AFSL:** 'Jayam' digital initiative. High reliance on field staff, but digital tools are improving efficiency. Data integrity issues noted in some reports.
*   **Branch & Network Expansion:**
    *   **CAGL:** 1,635 branches. Employee strength 10,770. 70% female workforce.
    *   **AFSL:** 292 branches. Targeting 661 branches by FY26. High attrition rates (25-30%) among field staff pose a risk.
*   **Collection Efficiency:**
    *   **CAGL:** ~97% collection efficiency. Strong recovery post-pandemic.
    *   **AFSL:** Collection efficiency dropped to ~58% in May-22 but recovering. Resolution 1.0 book shows high delinquency (>180 DPD at 42.79%).
*   **Management & Governance:**
    *   **CAGL:** Strong promoter holding (80.1%). Experienced management team.
    *   **AFSL:** Promoters with 30-year association. SAIF Partners nominee on board. Internal controls deemed adequate.

## Financial Deep Dive

*   **Revenue & Profitability:**
    *   **CAGL:** FY22 Net Profit ₹3.6bn. FY23E Net Profit ₹7.3-8.0bn. ROE projected at 18.3% for FY23E. NIM stable at 11.3-12.0%.
    *   **AFSL:** FY22 Consolidated Net Profit ₹31.7 Cr. Standalone PAT ₹162.3 Mn (up 103% YoY). High yields (22.9-26%) driven by MSME and 2W segments.
*   **Asset Quality:**
    *   **CAGL:** GNPA improved to 3.6% in FY22. Credit costs expected to normalize to 1.8-2.0%. NNPA near 0%.
    *   **AFSL:** GNPA rose to 7.7% (Standalone) in Q4 FY22 due to pandemic provisions and 2W segment stress. MSME GNPA at 7.9%. Projections suggest normalization to 3.4% by FY23E.
*   **Capital & Funding:**
    *   **CAGL:** CAR maintained at 23-29%. Strong liquidity buffers. QIP raised ₹8bn.
    *   **AFSL:** CAR target below 18%. High leverage (D/E 3-5.5x). Funding mix includes NCDs, banks, and DFIs. Recent CCD raise of ₹150 Cr.
*   **Growth Metrics:**
    *   **CAGL:** AUM growth 22% YoY in FY22. Guidance 24-25% for FY23E.
    *   **AFSL:** AUM growth 51% YoY in FY22. Target 40-50% for FY23E.

## Risk Matrix (Top 5 Risks)

1.  **Asset Quality Deterioration:**
    *   **Why it matters:** AFSL's GNPA spiked to 7.7% in Q4 FY22, driven by 2W and MSME segments. CAGL faces potential stress in Maharashtra and Karnataka.
    *   **Mitigation / Validation Ask:** Monitor monthly PAR trends and credit cost normalization. Validate AFSL's resolution strategies for Resolution 1.0 and 2.0 books.
2.  **Regulatory Changes:**
    *   **Why it matters:** Future RBI guidelines on income verification, interest rate caps, or data privacy could impact business models.
    *   **Mitigation / Validation Ask:** Engage with regulatory experts. Monitor RBI circulars closely. Assess impact of new MFI guidelines on 1QFY23.
3.  **Geographic Concentration:**
    *   **Why it matters:** AFSL has heavy exposure to Gujarat (49%) and MP (25%). CAGL has significant exposure to Karnataka, Maharashtra, and Tamil Nadu.
    *   **Mitigation / Validation Ask:** Diversification plans into UP, Bihar, and Rajasthan are critical. Validate execution of branch expansion in new states.
4.  **Funding & Liquidity Risk:**
    *   **Why it matters:** NBFC sector faces liquidity crunches. AFSL's high leverage (D/E 3-5.5x) increases refinancing risk.
    *   **Mitigation / Validation Ask:** Assess funding diversification and tenor matching. Monitor NCD issuance and bank line utilization.
5.  **Operational & Execution Risk:**
    *   **Why it matters:** Rapid expansion (AFSL targeting 661 branches) and integration of MMFL (CAGL) pose execution risks. High attrition rates in field staff.
    *   **Mitigation / Validation Ask:** Review HR policies and training programs. Validate IT system stability and integration progress.

## Red Flags & Warning Signs

*   **AFSL Asset Quality:** GNPA of 7.7% and high delinquency in Resolution 1.0 book (>180 DPD at 42.79%) are significant red flags. MSME segment GNPA at 7.9% indicates stress in this high-yield vertical.
*   **Data Integrity:** AFSL reports contain data gaps, '#REF!' errors, and missing values, raising concerns about data quality and reporting reliability.
*   **Concentration Risk:** AFSL's heavy reliance on Gujarat and MP, and CAGL's exposure to Karnataka and Maharashtra, creates regional risk.
*   **Funding Dependency:** AFSL's high leverage and reliance on NCDs and DFIs increase refinancing risk in a tight liquidity environment.
*   **Regulatory Uncertainty:** Potential for further regulatory changes in the MFI sector, particularly around income verification and pricing caps.
