## Executive Summary

**Verdict:** Hold

**Top 3 Reasons**
1. **Strong Growth & Profitability:** LoanTap has demonstrated exceptional growth with a 179% 3-year CAGR in AUM and achieved profitability by FY20, indicating a scalable and efficient business model.
2. **Strategic Pivot to LendTech:** The transition from a captive NBFC to a tech-led LendTech model with significant off-book lending (26% of AUM) diversifies risk and improves return metrics, aligning with modern fintech trends.
3. **Credit Quality Concerns:** Recent portfolio deterioration, with PAR 90 DPD peaking at 5.2% in late 2020 and collection efficiency volatility, poses a significant risk to the investment thesis and requires immediate validation.

## Strategic Fit & Market Opportunity

LoanTap targets the underserved urban prime millennial and retail MSME segments in India. The addressable market is vast, driven by increasing digital penetration and a credit gap in the formal lending ecosystem. The company's focus on 'prime' customers mitigates some credit risk compared to mass-market players, while its digital-first approach ensures low operational costs (Opex at 6% of revenue).

The market opportunity is further amplified by the company's B2B SaaS offering (LTFLoW), which allows it to monetize its technology stack beyond its own lending book. This dual-revenue model (lending + SaaS) provides a unique value proposition and potential for higher valuation multiples.

## Operational Due Diligence

**Technology & Underwriting:**
- Proprietary 'Finsome' scoring model uses multivariate data, shifting from traditional FOIR to score-based underwriting.
- 'Need-defined' user journey with flexible documentation enhances customer experience and conversion rates.
- High repeat customer ratios (87-99%) indicate strong product-market fit and customer loyalty.

**Acquisition & Channels:**
- Heavy reliance on digital and partnership channels for customer acquisition.
- CAC is low at 2.3% of loan size, supported by high LTV:CAC ratios (3x-10x).
- Risk of channel saturation or increased CAC in a competitive digital lending landscape.

**Funding & Liquidity:**
- Reliance on 22 external lenders for funding, with an average cost of funds of 14.5%.
- Leverage ratio is projected to be 2.8x by FY25, indicating a conservative capital structure.
- Credit rating of BBB- suggests access to institutional debt markets.

## Financial Deep Dive

**Historical Performance:**
- **Revenue Growth:** Revenue grew from INR 74.98 Cr in FY17-18 to INR 530.7 Cr in FY19-20, with YTD Q3 FY20-21 revenue at INR 423.56 Cr.
- **Profitability:** Achieved operating profit of INR 203.9 Cr in FY19-20, after a loss in FY17-18. YTD Q3 FY20-21 operating profit is INR 110.91 Cr.
- **Unit Economics:**
  - Salaried First-Time: Yield 22%, CoF 14.5%, Contribution Margin 11.43%
  - Salaried Repeat: Yield 22%, CoF 14.5%, Contribution Margin 13.93%
  - MSME First-Time: Yield 24%, CoF 14.5%, Contribution Margin 1.8%
  - MSME Repeat: Yield 24%, CoF 14.5%, Contribution Margin 4.3%

**Inferred Conclusions:**
- The company has successfully scaled its operations and achieved economies of scale, as evidenced by the high revenue growth and profitability.
- The contribution margins for MSME loans are significantly lower than salaried loans, indicating higher risk or cost in the MSME segment. However, repeat MSME customers show improved margins.
- The blended yield of ~22-24% provides a healthy spread over the 14.5% cost of funds, supporting strong NIMs (projected 14.2-14.4%).

## Risk Matrix (Top 5 Risks)

1. **Credit Quality Deterioration:** PAR 90 DPD peaked at 5.2% in Sep-Oct 2020, and collection efficiency dropped to 79.6% in Nov 2020. This indicates potential issues with underwriting or collection processes during the pandemic. **Mitigation:** Validate the root cause of the deterioration and assess the impact on future delinquency rates. Request detailed vintage analysis and recovery rates.
2. **Regulatory Risk:** The fintech lending sector in India is subject to evolving regulations, particularly regarding data privacy (Aadhaar, CIBIL) and digital lending practices. **Mitigation:** Monitor regulatory developments and ensure compliance with all applicable laws. Engage with legal counsel to assess potential impacts.
3. **Funding Liquidity Risk:** Reliance on 22 external lenders for funding creates concentration risk. Any disruption in funding could impact the company's ability to disburse loans. **Mitigation:** Diversify the lender base and secure long-term funding facilities. Assess the company's contingency plans for funding disruptions.
4. **Execution Risk:** Ambitious growth targets (21x AUM growth in 5 years) and the transition to a LendTech model require successful execution. **Mitigation:** Evaluate the management team's track record and the feasibility of the growth projections. Monitor key operational metrics closely.
5. **Channel Dependency Risk:** Heavy reliance on digital and partnership channels for acquisition may lead to saturation or increased CAC. **Mitigation:** Assess the sustainability of current acquisition channels and the company's plans for diversification. Monitor CAC trends and LTV:CAC ratios.

## Red Flags & Warning Signs

- **PAR 90 Spike:** The peak PAR 90 of 5.2% is a significant red flag, especially for a company targeting 'prime' customers. This suggests a potential flaw in the underwriting model or a deterioration in the economic environment affecting borrowers.
- **Collection Efficiency Volatility:** The drop in collection efficiency to 79.6% indicates challenges in debt recovery, which could impact cash flows and profitability.
- **Data Gaps:** Missing data in some tables (e.g., CAC table missing Q1FY21 data) hinders a complete analysis and raises questions about data integrity.
- **Complex Instrument Mix:** The presence of both Equity and CCPS across funding rounds requires careful analysis of rights and preferences, which may impact the investment structure.
